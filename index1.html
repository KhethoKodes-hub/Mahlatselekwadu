Her Story: Resilience Beyond Adversity

“My condition is not a disability — it is a different ability. Limitations can be transformed into purpose.”

Mahlatse Lekwadu – Entrepreneur, Co-Founder, MBA graduate (2021)

Mahlatse's journey is deeply rooted in resilience. Raised in a challenging environment, she took on responsibilities at a young age, helping care for her siblings and contributing to her household. These early experiences shaped her strength, independence, and determination.

Later in life, she faced one of her greatest challenges when she was diagnosed with Guillain-Barré Syndrome, a neurological condition that affected her mobility and basic functioning. During this time, she was unable to perform everyday tasks and became dependent on her children for care. She was also declared permanently incapacitated, a reality that took time to accept.

Despite these challenges, Mahlatse refused to let her condition define her. Through faith, perseverance, and rehabilitation, she rebuilt her strength, both physically and mentally. She describes her condition not as a disability, but as a “different ability”, a mindset that continues to guide her life and leadership.

After the passing of her husband — who founded the business — she stepped in to lead and grow the company, transforming it into a trusted supplier with established industry relationships.

Executive Snapshot

Resilient & Purpose-Driven Leadership

Mahlatse Lekwadu is a South African entrepreneur and Co-Founder of Magadine Business Enterprise. At 53, she has built a reputation as a resilient and purpose-driven leader in a traditionally male-dominated sector.

She holds a Postgraduate Diploma in Management and an MBA, which she completed in 2021 despite significant personal and health challenges. Following the passing of her husband, who founded the business, she stepped in to lead and grow the company, transforming it into a trusted supplier with established industry relationships. Her leadership is grounded in discipline, faith, and a commitment to excellence and inclusive economic participation.
